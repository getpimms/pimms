A/B testing is a must-have for improving conversions in 2025. It helps businesses [make](https://www.make.com/en) data-driven decisions, optimize user experiences, and boost ROI. Here’s a quick look at the top tools:

- **[PIMMS](https://pimms.io/)**: Affordable with a one-time $48 Pro plan, focused on [smart link tracking](/articles/utm-guide) and mobile app redirection.
- **[Optimizely](https://www.optimizely.com/)**: Advanced omnichannel testing, AI-driven personalization, but starts at $1,000/month.
- **[VWO](https://vwo.com/)**: Great for mid-sized businesses, with a free plan and pricing starting at $154/month.
- **[Kameleoon](https://www.kameleoon.com/)**: Enterprise-grade testing with strong analytics, starting around $25,000/year.
- **[AB Tasty](https://www.abtasty.com/)**: Tailored for enterprises, offering multi-armed bandit testing and personalization.

## 1\. [PIMMS](https://pimms.io/)

![PIMMS](https://assets.seobotai.com/pimms.io/684fdcd45559d477e759a931/5e0d1f98c86e809e83a56ff43417f965.jpg)

PIMMS is a [smart link-sharing](https://pimms.io) and [conversion tracking tool](https://pimms.io/blog) designed to ensure links open directly in the correct mobile apps - like YouTube, Amazon, or Vinted - helping to improve conversion rates.

For multi-channel campaigns, PIMMS simplifies the process by automatically tailoring user paths based on their device, eliminating the hassle of running separate tests.

### Experiment Types

PIMMS comes equipped with A/B testing features, letting you compare how different destination pages or links perform. It’s particularly effective for testing mobile app redirection strategies, helping you determine whether users engage more when directed to a web page or a native app. The platform supports testing across multiple channels, such as social media, email newsletters, SMS campaigns, and push notifications. You can also experiment with branded versus generic short links using custom domains to assess click-through rates and user trust.

All experiment data feeds directly into PIMMS's analytics system, ensuring every test provides actionable insights.

### Analytics & Reporting

PIMMS offers real-time dashboards that track key metrics like click-through rates, device types, locations, traffic sources, and conversions. You can apply advanced filters - using UTM parameters, device details, or geographic data - to turn raw numbers into meaningful insights.

On average, users see a 20–30% improvement in campaign performance [\[2\]](https://fibr.ai/conversion-rate-optimization/user-behavior-tools). Poor data quality, however, can result in significant losses, costing organizations around $12.9 million annually [\[2\]](https://fibr.ai/conversion-rate-optimization/user-behavior-tools).

Shared dashboards make it easy for teams to stay aligned by providing real-time updates. This collaborative feature allows multiple stakeholders to monitor campaign progress, boosting team efficiency by 15–25% and improving overall campaign results by 20–30% [\[2\]](https://fibr.ai/conversion-rate-optimization/user-behavior-tools).

### Integrations

PIMMS connects directly with essential revenue-tracking tools like [Stripe](https://stripe.com/), [Shopify](https://www.shopify.com/), [Zapier](https://zapier.com/), Make, n8n, and [Cal.com](https://cal.com/). These integrations bridge the gap between marketing efforts and revenue, giving you a full view of campaign ROI - not just surface-level metrics. Through [Zapier](https://zapier.com/) and Make, PIMMS can link with over 100 additional marketing and automation tools. This means you can set up workflows that trigger actions based on link performance, such as adding high-performing traffic sources to custom audiences or following up with users who clicked but didn’t convert.

It also integrates with scheduling tools, making it a great option for service-based businesses that need to track the entire customer journey - from the first click to booking an appointment or completing a form.

These integrations ensure that better tracking translates into more effective campaigns and higher ROI.

### Pricing

PIMMS offers flexible pricing plans to cater to businesses of various sizes:

- **Free Plan – Free**  
  Includes 10 smart links and basic click tracking with no credit card required.
- **Pro Plan – $48 (one-time payment)**  
  Provides unlimited smart links, clicks, and conversion events. This plan also includes A/B testing, team collaboration tools, custom domains, unlimited UTM parameters, QR code generation, and major integrations. It supports tracking up to $21,000 in monthly sales.
- **Business Plan – $53 per month**  
  Designed for growing businesses, this plan removes all limits. It includes unlimited sales tracking, priority support, enhanced reporting, and early access to new features.

This pricing structure makes PIMMS a great choice for small to medium-sized businesses looking for advanced tracking and testing capabilities without the high costs often associated with enterprise-level platforms.

## 2\. [Optimizely](https://www.optimizely.com/)

Optimizely is a platform designed to test and refine digital experiences across websites, mobile apps, and other digital channels. Optimizely offers a variety of experiment types to address different testing needs, including **A/B testing, multivariate testing, personalization, and feature management** [\[4\]](https://www.rightpoint.com/thought/article/what-is-optimizely-a-guide-to-the-leading-experimentation-platform). For web-based experiments, its Web Experimentation platform allows users to run A/B and multivariate tests, measuring their impact on key performance metrics. On the server side, its Feature Experimentation tool provides feature flagging and supports gradual rollouts.

The platform also uses AI-driven personalization to deliver tailored content and product recommendations [\[4\]](https://www.rightpoint.com/thought/article/what-is-optimizely-a-guide-to-the-leading-experimentation-platform). This is particularly relevant as over 75% of customers now expect personalized digital experiences [\[5\]](https://www.optimizely.com/insights/blog/the-future-of-digital-experience-optimization).

Optimizely supports testing across multiple channels, including web, mobile, email, and social media [\[5\]](https://www.optimizely.com/insights/blog/the-future-of-digital-experience-optimization). However, only **12% of experiments result in a winning variation** [\[6\]](https://www.optimizely.com/insights/blog/metrics-for-your-experimentation-program), highlighting the importance of rigorous testing to uncover successful strategies. Major brands using Optimizely have reported performance improvements ranging from 10% to 30% after leveraging its diverse testing capabilities [\[4\]](https://www.rightpoint.com/thought/article/what-is-optimizely-a-guide-to-the-leading-experimentation-platform).

### Analytics & Reporting

In 2024, they expanded their testing program from **40–50 tests annually to 600 variants**, completing 200 tests in the first quarter of 2025 alone [\[10\]](https://www.optimizely.com/insights/blog/test-and-learn-2025-key-takeaways). Elena Verna, a growth marketing expert, adds another perspective:
